Cabarete's Best Condo Neighborhoods

Not all of Cabarete is created equal. Each neighborhood delivers a different lifestyle, price point, and investment profile — here's the insider breakdown every buyer needs before making an offer.

🏖️ Most Walkable

Cabarete Bay

The beating heart of Cabarete. Condos here put you steps from 100+ restaurants, the famous beach bar strip, night markets, and the 5-kilometer golden beach. Highest demand = highest prices, but also highest rental occupancy rates. Perfect for buyers who want the full Caribbean beach town experience with everything on foot.

$180K – $925K+
🪁 #1 Kite Destination

Kite Beach

Ranked among the world's top 10 kite spots by National Geographic. Steady year-round trade winds, turquoise Atlantic water, and a parade of colorful kites overhead 300 days a year. Condos here command premium prices and attract a global buyer pool of watersports enthusiasts. Purpose-built developments like Ultravioleta Boutique Residences have set a new standard for luxury here.

$250K – $800K+
🌊 Upscale & Calm

Punta Goleta

The quieter, more sophisticated eastern flank of Cabarete Bay. Calmer waters make it ideal for swimming, paddleboarding, and families. Newer, larger-footprint condos with manicured communal spaces and 24-hour security. Attracts buyers looking for serene oceanfront living without the bustle of the main strip. Rapidly developing with several premium new builds underway.

$160K – $600K
🏄 Surf Capital

Playa Encuentro

One of the most actively gentrifying corners of North Coast real estate. Playa Encuentro is Cabarete's surf beach — consistent Atlantic swells, reef breaks, and a growing international surf community. Early buyers are securing undervalued condos and apartments before infrastructure improvements price the area up. Strong short-term rental demand from the surf tourism market.

$80K – $280K
🏡 Best Value

ProCab / Residential

The smart buyer's play. ProCab and surrounding residential neighborhoods sit a short drive from the beach but offer significantly lower price points, more space, and a genuine Dominican community feel. Popular with digital nomads, full-time expats, and investors looking to maximize square footage per dollar. Fully furnished 2-bedroom condos available from the low $100s.

$80K – $250K
🌴 Gated Communities

Sea Horse Ranch & Hilltop

For buyers prioritizing security, privacy, and prestige. Sea Horse Ranch is Cabarete's most exclusive address — a world-class gated equestrian community with private beach, stables, tennis, and sweeping oceanviews. Hillside condos and villas above Cabarete offer panoramic bay views and cooler temperatures. Entry price is higher, but the lifestyle is unmatched.

$300K – $2M+

Step-by-Step

How to Buy a Condo in Cabarete

The Dominican Republic makes foreign ownership surprisingly clean and straightforward — but knowing the process before you make an offer saves time, money, and surprises.

1

Define Your Budget & Goals

Personal use only? Pure investment? Mix of both? Your answer shapes everything — neighborhood, property type, and whether you buy furnished or unfurnished. Buyers using the property personally 1–3 months per year and renting the rest get the best of both worlds in Cabarete's market.

2

Hire a Dominican Attorney

Before you make any offers, retain an independent Dominican attorney (not the seller's). They'll conduct a title search (Certificado de Título), verify no liens or encumbrances exist, and handle the purchase contract. Budget $1,500–$3,000 USD for legal fees on a standard transaction.

3

Make an Offer & Sign Promise to Purchase

Once you've found your unit, make a written offer. If accepted, a Promise to Purchase (Promesa de Venta) is signed with a deposit — typically 10–20% of the purchase price. This legally binds both parties and sets the closing timeline (usually 30–60 days for a cash sale).

4

Wire Funds & Close

Wire remaining funds to escrow. Your attorney manages title transfer at the Registry of Titles (Registro de Títulos). Transfer tax (3% of assessed value) is paid at closing. You receive your Certificado de Título — your name, your property, under Dominican law.

5

List & Rent (Optional)

If renting, engage a local property management company (8–15% of rental revenue) or self-manage via Airbnb and VRBO. Most management companies in Cabarete handle guest communications, cleaning, and maintenance, making absentee ownership genuinely hands-off.

6

Apply for Investor Residency (Optional)

Purchases of $200,000+ qualify for the Dominican Investor Visa, giving you renewable legal residency. Many long-term buyers find the path to Dominican residency straightforward and useful for banking, driving, and healthcare access.

Investment Case

Why Cabarete Condos Generate Returns

Cabarete isn't just a beautiful place to own property — it's a structurally sound investment market. The combination of scarce beachfront supply, 500,000+ annual tourists, year-round watersport demand, and a growing digital nomad community creates rental demand that other Caribbean islands struggle to match.

The Dominican Republic's tax structure adds another layer of appeal: property taxes are modest (1% IPI on assessed value above ~$165K), there's no capital gains tax for individuals, and the DR peso's relative weakness against the dollar means foreign income stretches further. Condo prices on the North Coast have appreciated 5–10% annually over the last five years, with Kite Beach properties leading the trend.

Why Cabarete?

The Market Others Haven't Found Yet

Cabarete sits at the intersection of lifestyle and leverage. It's the only Caribbean destination that can genuinely claim to be the world capital of kite surfing and windsurfing — that gravitational pull of watersport tourism creates demand that doesn't evaporate between resort seasons.

🌍

Global Buyer Community

Cabarete's buyer pool spans US, Canadian, German, French, Swiss, Scandinavian, and Latin American investors — an established, internationally minded community that's been building here for 20+ years. Cabarete's English-heavy expat scene makes the buying process less intimidating than other DR markets.

📶

Digital Nomad Infrastructure

Reliable fiber internet, co-working spaces, an active remote-worker community, and a cost of living of $1,500–$2,500/month for couples make Cabarete one of the Caribbean's top digital nomad hubs. This sustains year-round rental demand that purely resort-driven markets don't enjoy.

🛡️

Secure Foreign Ownership

The Dominican Republic constitutionally guarantees equal property rights for foreign buyers. There's no "nominee" structure required, no forced local partnership, no repatriation restrictions on proceeds. Your title is your title — registered, recorded, and legally protected at the national level.

🌪️

Geography & Climate

Cabarete's location on the DR's northern coast places it largely outside the main Atlantic hurricane corridor. The consistent trade winds that make it a kite surfing mecca also deliver reliably mild temperatures year-round. No wetsuit required, ever. Climate risk is real across the Caribbean — Cabarete is better positioned than most.

✈️

20-Minute Airport Access

Gregorio Luperón International Airport (POP) in Puerto Plata is 20 minutes from central Cabarete — direct flights from Miami, New York, Toronto, Montreal, and multiple European cities. The ease of access is a key driver of rental demand and what makes Cabarete work as a destination for short-term rentals.

📈

5–10% Annual Appreciation

North Coast DR condo prices have appreciated 5–10% annually over the past five years according to Global Property Guide data. Kite Beach and Cabarete Bay properties have outpaced the market. Early entry into developing pockets like Playa Encuentro positions buyers for above-average capital gains on the next cycle.
